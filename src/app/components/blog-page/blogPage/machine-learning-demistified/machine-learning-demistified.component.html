Will I go to University?

by Mikey Fennelly. February 4th, 2024

The short answer to this question is, maybe.

Of course my parents are keen for me to get a University qualification and I do understand the value of that (company hiring policies etc), but I'm also quite impatient about getting to work in the real world, on real projects and with experienced Engineers.

Universities have their advantages (and are fun), but I'm not crazy about the idea of sitting in classrooms and lecture theatres for 4 or 5 years before I get stuck into real world work. I'm confident that I can learn a lot faster, especially if I'm in the right environment where what I'm doing really matters and I'm doing it with really good Engineers. I know that's a much better and faster way for me to learn!

So then why do I say "maybe"?

There is one University course in Ireland that I think would be well suited to me and what I want to do. That course is Immersive Software Engineering at the University of Limerick. While on this course, students get to spend a lot of time "immersed" in real work environments in really good companies and they start doing it in their 1st year. I really do like the sound of this course and if I could get into it next year I'd definitely do it.
